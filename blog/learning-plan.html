Cultural Blog - Learning Plan for Bootcamp


What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

I was thinking of working in the tech field, possibly on the venture capitalism side of things. Side projects would be looking into the burgeoning field of Quantum Computing.


What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

Strengths: problem solving in a group
Limitations: Dealing with difficult people, but then again I do have a lot of experience with that.


What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

Honestly, in our society in general there are too many one-upmanship type characters, which just makes it all less fun. Everyone has strengths and weaknesses and the most optimal group is that one that can leverage on all the different strengths, but the pervasive one-upmanship mentality do often get in the way.


What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

How to gain the trust of others without compromising on any of my values. I'm from Singapore and the common narrative is that we are a draconian society, despite actually having far fewer people serving a sentence or bail order; a far higher successful rehab rate; fewer police shooting people and people shooting police incidents; fewer homeless people; higher home-ownership rates; better access to education and other resources amongst the lower socioeconomic households; much lower crime rate etc. Often people are caught up on our chewing gum laws though, even though there's more life than that. I've had to compromise on my values quite a bit just to be able to have a word in.


What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

I'm actually very happy with the support from the facilitators thus far! To be available for technical and human support really.


What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp

Learn and practice a lot of useful tech and human skills.

I am naturally very inquisitive and reflective but not very focused. Ironically in the inquisitiveness that causes me to drift away often in my thoughts and get caught up researching and reading up on other topics whilst I still have a distinct learning 'curriculum' to get through.

To be able to communicate my ideas more clearly and not just be content with being the learner all the time.

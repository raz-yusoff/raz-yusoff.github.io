Problem Solving

A time I got stuck on a simple problem

I didn't really get stuck anywhere, just frustrated at one part because the instructions kinda threw me off lol. In the JS-Olympics repl.it, the instructions for the Bluk Up part asked us to use a for loop, so I used a for loop and it worked. But then at the end of the whole challenge I looked at the sample solution for referrence and noticed they used a forEach loop! I would have considered using a forEach loop if that hadn't thrown me off like that. But not really that angry about it. Ah well as long as it works and I'm learning.

A time I solved a problem in an elegant way

In the fizzBuzz challenge I completely forgot about the remainder operator (%) and used a sequence of for loops and if loops. It works and I guess I pretty much hard corded the remainder operator with my loops. When I refered to the sample solutions afterwards and noticed they just used the % operator, I stuck to my guns and declared that my solution is pretty elegant, in the sense that imagine if I was dealing with a programming language that didn't have the remainder operator but had the usual for iteration loops and if/else statement functionality, means that I would still be able to do the fizzBuzz challenge lol.

Reflect on how confident you feel using the problem solving techniques and process:

  • Pseudocode: very happy with learning this method. It has helped me tremendously and if you look at my repl.it excercises I use this method quite a bit.

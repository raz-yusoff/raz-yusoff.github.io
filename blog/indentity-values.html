Share an example from your experience of where you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss the strategies you used at that time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now, and why.

  • Plenty of times with various government departments. They are too rigid and not diverse enough to be able to learn from different systems. Coming from my cultural background, often the first challenge is to justify that you’re not speaking from an irrational place without any data or logic to back you up. The problem is when people have the tendency to view themselves as 'logical' when all they're doing is limiting their understanding of life to the simplest logics, while crazily at the same time acknowledging that the world doesn't seem to be adhering to those logics. Life is a complex web of micro-logics and if you're the type who thinks that the world can be explained by simple blocky chuncks of logic, chances are you're way off. I find that people and countries with a more international, diverse background tend to be able to understand this better and use it to their advantage.
